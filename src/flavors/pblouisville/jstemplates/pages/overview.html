About Idea Collection

For the first time in Louisville Metro, community members – like you – will directly decide how to spend $150,000 of the public budget in Metro Council Districts 6 and 8. Ideas will be collected until October 21 and assessed for feasibility, equity, and cost by resident volunteers from November-January. In March, residents will vote and the top ideas will be funded until the full $75,000 (per district) has been spent.

As a resident of your neighborhood, you know your community best. Submit ideas that improve your community’s health and mental wellness. Each district will spend $50,000 from their Councilmember’s budget which must be allocated for physical projects, meaning they must be “built.” This can include park benches, roadways, signs, murals, neighborhood repairs, and more. The additional $25,000 can be used for these kinds of physical projects or other ideas you may have.

Who can participate:

Anyone represented by Council President David James or Councilmember Brandon Coan can submit an idea.

The eligible neighborhoods for CM James include Algonquin, California, Limerick, Old Louisville, Park Hill, Russell, Taylor-Berry, University, and Victory Park.

The eligible neighborhoods for CM Coan include Belknap, Bonnycastle, Braeview, Cherokee Seneca – Alta Vista, Cherokee Triangle, Deer Park, Gardiner Lane – Upper Highlands, Hawthorne, Hayfield Dundee – Upper Highlands, Original Highlands, Tyler Park. Eligible cities include Kingsley, Seneca Gardens, Strathmoor Manor, and Strathmoor Village.
